This tutorial introduces the mathematical language at the heart of quantum mechanics and quantum computing — Dirac (bra-ket) notation.

## What You Will Learn

- Why physicists and quantum computing researchers use bra-ket notation
- How kets |ψ⟩ represent quantum states as vectors in Hilbert space
- How bras ⟨ψ| are linear functionals (dual vectors)
- Inner products, orthonormality, and the Born rule
- Completeness relations and basis expansions
- Outer products and projection operators

## Prerequisites

Familiarity with vectors and matrices from a first linear algebra course. No prior quantum mechanics knowledge required.

## How to Use This Tutorial

Work through the notes first, then attempt the exercises at the end. Solutions are available in office hours. If you are stuck, pay close attention to the notation — most errors come from confusing kets with their corresponding bras.
