A rigorous treatment of calculus extended to two and three dimensions. Students develop fluency with partial derivatives, multiple integrals, and the classical theorems of vector calculus — essential tools for physics, engineering, and advanced mathematics.

## Topics Covered

- Functions of several variables, level curves, and surfaces
- Limits, continuity, and partial derivatives
- The gradient, directional derivatives, and linearization
- The multivariable chain rule
- Optimization: critical points, second derivative test, Lagrange multipliers
- Double and triple integrals in Cartesian, polar, cylindrical, and spherical coordinates
- Line integrals and surface integrals
- Green's Theorem, Stokes' Theorem, and the Divergence Theorem

## Prerequisites

Single-variable calculus (limits, derivatives, integrals, sequences and series) and basic analytic geometry. Familiarity with vectors is helpful but not required.
