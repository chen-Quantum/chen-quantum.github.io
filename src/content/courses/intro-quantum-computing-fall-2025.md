An accessible introduction to quantum computation for students with linear algebra and probability backgrounds. No prior physics knowledge is assumed — every concept is built rigorously from the mathematics, with physical intuition developed alongside.

## Topics Covered

- Classical computation and its limits
- Qubits, quantum states, and Dirac notation
- Single-qubit gates: Hadamard, Pauli, phase gates
- Multi-qubit systems, tensor products, and entanglement
- Bell states and the EPR paradox
- Quantum circuits and universality
- Key algorithms: Deutsch-Jozsa, Grover's search, Shor's factoring (overview)
- Introduction to quantum error correction

## Prerequisites

Linear algebra (vectors, matrices, inner products, eigenvalues) is essential. Comfort with complex numbers and basic probability is also expected.

## Format

Two lectures per week plus a weekly tutorial session. The course uses Qiskit (Python) for circuit simulation — no prior quantum physics required, but prior Python experience is a plus.
