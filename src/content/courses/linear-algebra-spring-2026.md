This course covers the fundamentals of linear algebra with an emphasis on geometric intuition and computational fluency. Students build a rigorous understanding of vector spaces, linear transformations, and matrix decompositions — tools that underpin everything from data science to quantum mechanics.

## Topics Covered

- Vector spaces, subspaces, span, and basis
- Linear independence and dimension
- Matrix operations, inverses, and determinants
- Eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and diagonalization
- Orthogonality and the Gram-Schmidt process
- Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)
- Applications: least squares, principal component analysis, Markov chains

## Prerequisites

Single-variable calculus and basic set notation. Comfort with algebraic manipulation is expected. Proof-writing experience is helpful but will be developed throughout the course.

## Format

Weekly tutorials are 50 minutes. Practice sheets are released each week and reviewed in tutorial. Assignments are submitted via the course portal.
